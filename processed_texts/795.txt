The chart given below provides data about development of the book market in one selected year and gives predictions concerning 2018. Overall, the tendency is ambigious and it is not clear, whether eBook will occupy the world market.
Firstly, it is obvious that the USA was a leader in selling of printed books in 2014 and the number of the sold books accounted for 10,5 billion Dollars, whereas the quantity of eBooks was two times as small. In contrast to the USA, Germany's book market could sell only a half of the USA's number of printed books. The UK shows the lowest results: the realization of printed books in the UK made up 2,6 billion Dollars, while China overtook Great Britain twofolds. As for the future, only two countries tend to retain printed book versions. Germany and China belong to them, whereas the USA and the UK show opposite results: selling of printed books will be slightly reduced in 2018.
In conclusion, we see that in both english-speaking countries the number of ebooks will outweight their alternatives in2018.
