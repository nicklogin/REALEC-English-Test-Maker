The bar chart depicts the book market situation in different countries in 2014 with a forecast for the year of 2018. 	The graph obviously shows the domination of the american "book national product". Nearly all the bars are hovering high above the statistics of the other competitors. Furthermore, the USA print book market in 2014 is the highest critical point in the chart. On the other hand, it is pinpointed that production of printed fiction and documentary sources in the United Kingdom is at the lowest point compared to the other countries.
The most noticeable trend is the rise of eBooks popularity in all the countries.
For example, in Germany it is believed that the investments in this sphere would be multiplied by 3 from 0.5 to 1.5 billions USD by the year of 2018. We can also spot the obvious dawn of the printed sources. All countries with the exception of Germany are expected to reduce the development of this book market in the given timeline. In China the changes, however, are not as critical: from 4.8 to 4.2.
Overall, the bar chart illustrates us two trends occuring in all of this countries from the rise of electronic reading and the downfall of the traditional one, as well as the statistics of the biggest and smallest book markets among them.
