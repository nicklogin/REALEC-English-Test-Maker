The graph depict the development of print and eBook market in the USA, Germany, China and the UK.
The most striking feature is that the USA have the most developed book market in 2014 and 2018. The sales of print issues have fallen from 10,5 billion dollars to 7,5 but eBook sales have increased from 5,5 to 8,5 million dollars in the USA. Germany is on the second place for the printed books: their incomes have been the same in two years - 6 billion dollars. EBook production has slowly risen from 1 to 1,5 billion dollars. China has a high level of printed book sales: it was 4,8 billion in 2014 and 4,2 in 2018. The incomes from eBook have been smaller - 0,5 in 2014 and 1 in 2018. And the UK has nearly the eaquel incomes from eBooks and printed books. The dynamic of sales reflects the common changes of incomes.
All in all, the chart shows that almost in all countries the development of printed books has reduced but in spite of this fact it is higher than the incomes of eBooks, which sales have increased slowly.
